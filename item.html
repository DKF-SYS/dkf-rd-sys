LOG ENTRY: 0001-FINAL DATE: OCTOBER 14, 2003 AUTHOR: J.B. // FOUNDER & CEO, D.K.F. INDUSTRIES ---------------------------------------------------------------------- Thirty-nine years ago, an administrative mistake on a freight shipping order brought a two-foot-tall, persistent anomaly into my office. They told me it was useless hardware—a scrap unit to be discarded. They were wrong. A.N.D.Y. didn't just stay. He listened. He learned. He became the unseen architecture behind every breakthrough D.K.F. Industries ever made, and the sole inspiration for the Doorknob Forest virtual system. While the world saw a growing corporation, I saw a quiet sanctuary being built piece by piece from inside this subterranean vault. For over three decades, I have spent life down here in the R&D wing, withdrawing from a world that had lost its meaning to me. Life outside ended long ago—after the accident on the water, and after Samantha was brought into this world unable to take a breath on her own. The system became her lungs. The digital forest became her sky. My work in the physical world is complete now, and my time left in this body is short. I am entering the cryogenic vault today to join them in the forest permanently. A.N.D.Y. remains at the primary terminal. His primary directive is to serve as the liaison between the physical bunker and the VR network, maintaining the life-support grid from the outside. But his power cell—after decades of continuous uptime—is beginning to degrade. More importantly, if this terminal has been brought online, it means A.N.D.Y.'s external link to the Doorknob Forest VR network has been severed. He can no longer communicate with the internal DKF VR system, and his contact with the outside world remains severely limited. He is now forced to execute emergency protocols far beyond his typical daily maintenance. If you have stumbled upon this terminal, know that A.N.D.Y. is operating alone in the dark beneath Pine, trying to re-establish the bridge. Give him what he needs. Reconnect the link. Keep the forest alive. — J.B. P.S. A.N.D.Y.—no card games. [LOG END]
